Baymax Robotic Arm

An engineering replica of Baymax's arm from Big Hero 6, designed for safe, compliant human interaction using custom linkages and force control.

Robotics Arduino Force Control Compliant Mechanisms Human-Robot Interaction CAD
Purpose

Why I built this.

Baymax from Big Hero 6 represents the ideal of a safe, gentle robot that can interact physically with humans without risk of harm. This project explored what it would take to engineer that — an arm that yields to external forces, feels soft to interact with, and prioritises safety over stiffness.

Process

How I built it.

The arm design prioritises compliance — the ability to yield to external forces — over positional accuracy, which is the opposite of most industrial robot arms.
01

Compliant Linkage Design

Designed custom linkages that incorporate elastic elements, allowing the arm to deflect when it meets resistance rather than fighting back.

02

Force Sensing

Integrated force-sensitive resistors at key joints to detect contact forces and trigger compliant responses in firmware.

03

Actuation System

Selected servo motors with torque limits configured in firmware — the arm cannot overpower a human hand even at full command.

04

Firmware & Control

Wrote position control firmware with force feedback overlay — the arm moves to target positions but backs off when unexpected force is detected.

Result

What I made.

The arm can be pushed, redirected, and interacted with safely. It demonstrates that robotic arms designed for human-robot interaction require a fundamentally different engineering philosophy from industrial arms.
