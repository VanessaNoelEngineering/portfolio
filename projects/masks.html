Replica Props

3D printed replicas built for the love of it — Zelda masks and a life-sized Minecraft chicken. Multi-part CAD, FDM printing, and hand-finishing from start to display.

3D Printing CAD Modelling Prop Making Hand Finishing Painting FDM
Purpose

Why I built these.

Not every project needs a practical application. These were built because they're fun — and because the process of faithfully recreating something you love is genuinely challenging. Majora's Mask and the Deku Mask required close attention to shape, colour blocking, and surface detail to feel true to the originals. The Minecraft Chicken required the opposite discipline: embracing deliberate blockiness at real-world scale. Both pushed the limits of what FDM printing and post-processing can achieve.

Process

How I built them.

Each piece followed the same core loop: reference → model → print → finish. The challenge was different for each.
01

Reference & Proportions

Collected high-resolution references from every angle — in-game screenshots, official art, merchandise, and fan documentation. Established accurate proportions before touching the CAD.

02

Multi-Part CAD Modelling

Modelled each piece in interlocking parts — split by colour region where possible to avoid painting over printed detail, and designed with tolerances for clean assembly without hardware.

03

FDM Printing

Printed in the appropriate base filament colours for each section. Orientation and support placement were carefully chosen to minimise visible layer lines on hero surfaces.

04

Hand Finishing

Sanded, primed, and painted each piece by hand. The masks received layered paint, weathering, and a seal coat. The chicken stayed raw-printed to preserve the blocky aesthetic — no smoothing.

Result

What I made.

Three display-quality prop replicas. The Zelda masks capture the character of the originals through careful painting and finishing — the 3D printer gets you 80% of the way, skilled hand finishing gets you the rest. The Minecraft Chicken is exactly what it sounds like: a giant blocky chicken that brings joy to everyone who sees it.
