Majora's & Deku Masks

Faithful replicas of the iconic masks from The Legend of Zelda — multi-part modelled, printed, and hand-finished to game-accurate detail.

3D Printing CAD Modelling Prop Making Hand Finishing Painting Hobby
Purpose

Why I built this.

Two of the most iconic objects in gaming — Majora's Mask and the Deku Mask from The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask. The goal was faithful recreation: not just the shape, but the colour blocking, surface texture, and intricate detail of each mask as it appears in the game and official art.

Process

How I built it.

Each mask was treated as a prop-making challenge — precision modelling followed by careful post-processing and hand painting.
01

Reference Gathering

Collected high-resolution reference images from multiple angles — in-game, promotional art, and official merchandise — to establish accurate proportions.

02

Multi-Part CAD Modelling

Modelled each mask in multiple interlocking parts to allow printing in different colours and avoid complex support structures.

03

Printing & Assembly

Printed each part in the appropriate base filament colour. Assembled with precision fits designed into the model.

04

Surface Finishing

Sanded, primed, and painted each mask by hand — layering colours, adding weathering, and sealing with a finish coat.

Result

What I made.

Two display-quality prop replicas that capture the character of the originals. The project was a deep dive into the intersection of digital fabrication and traditional craft — the 3D printer gets you 80% of the way, and skilled hand finishing gets you the rest.
