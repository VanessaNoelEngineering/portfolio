Mechanical Flower

A robotic lotus that blossoms when touched. Capacitive sensing, servo actuation, and compliant mechanisms combine for a smooth, organic bloom.

Robotics Arduino Servo Control Capacitive Sensing Mechanisms CAD
Purpose

Why I built this.

The goal was to create a kinetic sculpture that feels alive — a flower that responds to human touch with a natural, organic blooming motion. The design challenge was making mechanical actuation feel soft and biological rather than robotic and rigid.

Process

How I built it.

The design combined electrical sensing, mechanical linkage design, and firmware to create a seamless touch-to-bloom experience.
01

Petal Linkage Design

Designed four-bar linkages for each petal that produce a curved, organic sweep rather than a linear open motion. Iterated in CAD before printing.

02

Capacitive Touch Sensing

Integrated capacitive touch electrodes at the base of the stem. When a hand touches the stem, the sensor triggers the bloom sequence.

03

Servo Integration

Selected and mounted micro-servos to drive each petal linkage. Tuned the servo sweep angles for a smooth, slow opening motion.

04

Arduino Firmware

Wrote the control firmware — touch detection, servo sequencing, timing curves — to make the bloom feel natural rather than mechanical.

Result

What I made.

The flower opens slowly and gracefully when touched, holds open for a moment, then gently closes. The organic feel of the motion is what makes it special — visitors often touch it multiple times just to watch it bloom again.
