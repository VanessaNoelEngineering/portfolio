Gesture Controlled Car

An Arduino RC car controlled entirely by wrist gestures — no buttons, no joystick. Tilt your hand forward to accelerate, lean left to turn.

Arduino C++ IMU Wireless Comms Firmware Embedded Systems Sensor Fusion
Purpose

Why I built this.

The goal was to explore intuitive, gesture-based control for a physical system — removing the abstraction layer of buttons and joysticks and making the control feel direct. Tilting your wrist to steer a car feels natural in a way that pushing a joystick doesn't. This project was an exploration of that human-machine interface idea using accessible hardware.

Process

How I built it.

The system has two halves: a wearable IMU controller and the car itself, communicating wirelessly in real time.
01

IMU Controller Build

Built a wrist-worn controller around an MPU-6050 IMU (accelerometer + gyroscope) and an Arduino with a wireless transmitter.

02

Sensor Fusion

Implemented a complementary filter to combine accelerometer and gyroscope data for stable, drift-free orientation readings.

03

Gesture Mapping

Mapped pitch (forward/back tilt) to throttle and roll (left/right tilt) to steering angle. Added dead zones to prevent unintentional commands.

04

Wireless Protocol

Implemented a lightweight wireless protocol between controller and car — fast enough for real-time control, robust to packet loss.

05

Car Integration

Integrated the receiver into the RC car chassis, replacing the original controller input with the gesture signal. Tuned response curves for natural feel.

Result

What I made.

A fully functional gesture-controlled car that responds naturally to wrist movements. The control feels intuitive after about 30 seconds of use — the learning curve is minimal because the mapping is physical and direct.
