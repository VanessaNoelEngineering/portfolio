Giant LEGO Minifigure

A fully 3D printed, 10× scale model of a LEGO Classic Space Minifigure. Every joint articulates exactly like the original — no glue, no hardware.

3D Printing SolidWorks FDM Tolerance Engineering Hobby
Purpose

Why I built this.

As a devoted LEGO fan, I wanted to recreate a LEGO Classic Space Minifigure (set 71037) at 10× scale while keeping it fully posable and 100% 3D printed — no adhesives or additional hardware. The challenge was engineering every joint to snap together and move just like the original, just bigger.

Process

How I built it.

The project went through two major scale iterations before arriving at the final 10× model, with each phase refining tolerances and adding detail.
01

CAD Modelling

Used SolidWorks to recreate every part of the minifigure from scratch, scaled to 10× the original dimensions. Reference calipers on the real piece were used extensively.

02

5× Prototype Print

Printed a half-scale (5×) version first to validate proportions and joint fits before committing to full-scale printing time.

03

Tolerance Refinement

Adjusted snap-fit tolerances based on prototype feedback. FDM printing introduces shrinkage and layer artifacts that affect fit — multiple iterations were needed per joint.

04

Detail Pass

Added surface details — chest logos, helmet visor geometry, face details — at full 10× scale once proportions were confirmed.

05

Full Scale Print

Printed all parts at 10× scale across approximately 90 hours of total print time, in multiple filament colours matching the original.

Result

What I made.

The final minifigure is fully posable — every joint rotates exactly as the original. The project was a lesson in tolerance engineering at scale: what works at 1× does not automatically work at 10×.
