Satellite Launches

Small-scale satellites launched to the stratosphere via QSET, monitoring critical payload metrics throughout flight and returning data for mission analysis.

Aerospace Embedded Systems Arduino PCB Design Python Data Analysis CSA
Purpose

Why I built this.

Through the Queens Space Engineering Team (QSET) in partnership with the Canadian Space Agency, I helped design, build, and launch small satellites into the stratosphere. The mission: collect and analyse payload metrics — temperature, pressure, altitude, acceleration — throughout the flight envelope to improve future missions and give students real aerospace engineering experience.

Process

How I built it.

Each satellite went through a full engineering lifecycle — design, build, test, launch, recover, analyse — compressed into a student program timeline.
01

Satellite Design

Designed the satellite structure, sensor payload, and data logging system. Constraints: survive stratospheric temperatures, fit within weight limits, and survive landing impact.

02

PCB & Electronics

Designed and soldered custom PCBs integrating temperature, pressure, altitude, and IMU sensors with an Arduino-based data logger.

03

Pre-Launch Testing

Conducted thermal testing, vibration testing, and end-to-end data logging validation before each launch.

04

Launch & Recovery

Launched via weather balloon to the stratosphere. Tracked via GPS. Recovered after descent for data retrieval.

05

Data Analysis

Analysed collected payload data in Python — graphing flight profiles, identifying anomalies, and writing mission reports to improve future iterations.
