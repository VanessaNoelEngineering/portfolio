Happy Tappy

An open-source, modular EMDR tapper making Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing therapy more affordable and accessible.

Embedded Systems Arduino Open Source Healthcare Accessibility WIP
Purpose

Why I built this.

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) therapy is an evidence-based treatment for trauma and PTSD. It uses bilateral stimulation — alternating taps on each hand — delivered by a device called a tapper. Commercial EMDR tappers can cost hundreds of dollars, putting them out of reach for many therapists and patients. Happy Tappy is a fully open-source tapper designed to cost a fraction of that while being modular, customisable, and easy to build.

Process

How I built it.

The design prioritises accessibility on two levels: affordable enough to build, and simple enough to modify and repair.
01

Requirements Research

Researched clinical requirements for EMDR tappers — frequency range, tactile feedback characteristics, bilateral timing accuracy — to ensure the device is therapeutically appropriate.

02

Hardware Design

Designed a minimal hardware platform: microcontroller, two vibration motor drivers, a simple UI for frequency/intensity control. All off-the-shelf components.

03

Firmware Development

Wrote the bilateral stimulation firmware — precise alternating timing, smooth frequency sweeps, intensity control — targeting therapeutic frequency ranges.

04

Open Source Release

Published all hardware designs, firmware, and assembly instructions under an open licence so anyone can build, modify, or improve the design.
